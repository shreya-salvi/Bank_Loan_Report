Key findings
Income gap is massive — approved applicants earn $144.7K on average versus $66.2K for rejected, a 2.2× difference. Income below $40K results in 0% approval.
CD Account is the strongest binary signal — holders are approved at 46.4% vs 7.2% for non-holders. This likely reflects broader wealth rather than a direct causal factor.
Education + Income dominate predictions — together they account for 73% of the Decision Tree's feature importance, making them the two critical variables.
Credit card spend is a strong proxy — approved applicants spend $3,910/mo on average vs $1,730/mo. This may reflect lifestyle and repayment capacity.
Age has no predictive power — approval rates are flat across all age groups (8.7%–10.8%), suggesting age-based discrimination would be both unfair and inaccurate.
Family size has a moderate effect — families of 3–4 are approved at ~12% vs 7–8% for singles, contributing 18.6% of the Decision Tree's importance.
Decision Tree significantly outperforms Logistic Regression — 98.7% vs 95.4% accuracy, with far fewer false negatives (12 vs 34). Use DT for production.
High-risk segment — low income + undergrad education + no CD account = near-zero approval probability. This group represents the majority of rejected applicants.
